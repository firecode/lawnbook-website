Job Scheduling

⏱ 8 min read Updated March 2025

The Schedule tab is where your workday takes shape. It combines a visual calendar, job creation, status tracking, and weather data into one view so you always know what's happening and when.

Calendar View

Tap Schedule in the tab bar to open the calendar. By default, you'll see a weekly view with jobs displayed as color-coded blocks.

Calendar view modes

  • Day view — shows all jobs for a single day in chronological order with time blocks. Best for busy days.
  • Week view — shows the current week at a glance. Tap any day column to jump to day view.
  • Month view — shows job counts per day with colored dots indicating statuses. Good for planning ahead.
  • List view — shows jobs as a flat list, sorted by date. Useful for searching or bulk-editing.

Swipe left or right to move between days, weeks, or months depending on which view mode you're in. Tap Today in the top-left corner to jump back to the current date.

Tip: On iPad and Mac, the calendar uses a split-pane layout. The calendar stays visible on the left while you edit job details on the right — no need to navigate back and forth.

Creating Jobs

You can create a job from three places: the calendar, a client profile, or the + button in the tab bar.

1

Tap + New Job or tap and hold on a time slot in day view to create a job at that time.

2

Search for and select the client. Then choose the property from their profile (most clients have one).

3

Select the service type — mowing, trimming, fertilizing, cleanup, or a custom service. You can select multiple services for the same visit.

4

Set the date and time. The duration is estimated automatically based on lot size, but you can override it.

5

Set a price for the job. You can price each service line separately or enter a flat job total.

6

Add internal notes (not visible to clients) and tap Save Job.

Job color coding

Jobs on the calendar are color-coded by service type so you can read your day at a glance. Mowing is green, fertilizing is yellow, cleanup is orange, and so on. Colors are customizable in Settings.

Recurring Services

When creating or editing a job, toggle Recurring to set up a repeating series. The job you're editing becomes the first occurrence.

Recurrence options

  • Weekly — same day every week. Most common for mowing clients.
  • Every 2 weeks — alternating weeks. Good for bi-weekly mowing.
  • Every 3 weeks — common in spring and fall when growth slows.
  • Monthly — good for fertilizing, aeration, or seasonal services.
  • Custom — set any interval in days.

When you edit a recurring job, LawnBook asks whether you want to change this job only, this and all future jobs, or all jobs in the series. This prevents accidental changes from affecting your whole season.

Tip: For seasonal contracts (e.g., March through October), set the end date when creating the recurring series. LawnBook will stop generating jobs automatically.

Job Statuses

Every job moves through a lifecycle as work is completed and payment is collected. LawnBook tracks this automatically as you take action.

Scheduled

The job is on the calendar for a future date, or today but not yet started. Default state when you create a job.

In Progress

You've tapped Start Job or clocked in. The clock is running and the job appears at the top of your active list.

Completed

You've tapped Complete Job. Time is recorded, and the job is ready to be invoiced. LawnBook prompts you to create an invoice.

Invoiced

An invoice has been created and sent for this job. The job is linked to the invoice and payment tracking begins.

You can also manually set a job to Cancelled or Skipped. Cancelled jobs are removed from route calculations. Skipped jobs (client away, weather) are logged but don't count as missed.

Weather-Aware Scheduling

LawnBook checks the forecast for each job's location and warns you when weather could affect service delivery.

⛈

Rain Warning: 2 jobs scheduled for Thursday show a rain probability above 70%. Tap to see affected jobs and reschedule options.

How weather warnings work

  • LawnBook fetches a 10-day forecast for each job's property address.
  • Jobs scheduled on days with >60% rain probability receive a yellow weather badge on the calendar.
  • Jobs scheduled on days with >80% rain probability receive a red warning — the Daily Briefing will call these out by name.
  • Tap the weather badge on any job to see the full forecast for that location.
  • Tap Reschedule to move the job to the next available clear day.

Tip: Weather data is fetched when your device has internet. LawnBook caches the last-known forecast for offline use, though it may not reflect updates if you've been offline for more than 12 hours.

Managing Your Daily Schedule

The Dashboard shows today's jobs in route-optimized order. You can also view your full day from the Schedule tab in Day view.

Reordering today's jobs

In Day view or the Dashboard job list, press and hold any job, then drag it to a new position. The route recalculates automatically to show your updated driving time and order.

Marking jobs complete in the field

  • Open the job from the Dashboard or calendar, then tap Start Job when you arrive.
  • Add any completion notes, take a before/after photo, or log supply usage.
  • Tap Complete Job when finished. LawnBook records the actual duration.
  • Choose whether to Create Invoice Now or defer invoicing to the end of the day.

End-of-day review

At the end of each day, you'll receive a summary notification listing completed jobs, total earnings, pending invoices, and tomorrow's schedule. Tap it to open the day summary in LawnBook.
