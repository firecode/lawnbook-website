Step 9 — AI Tools & Assistant

Your AI Lawn Care Expert, Running Right on Your Phone

LawnBook Guide · 9 min read
All AI features run entirely on your device. Nothing is ever uploaded.

LawnBook includes a full suite of AI-powered tools designed specifically for lawn care professionals. Every feature — from the conversational assistant to photo analysis — runs using on-device machine learning. No internet connection required, no data leaving your phone.

Lawn Assistant Chat

The Lawn Assistant is a conversational AI you can ask anything about lawn care, your business, or your schedule. Access it from the AI tab or by tapping the assistant icon in the top corner of any screen.

Questions you can ask:

  • "What's the best time to apply pre-emergent in Georgia?"
  • "How much fertilizer should I apply to a 5,000 sq ft Bermuda lawn?"
  • "Which of my clients haven't had service in over 30 days?"
  • "What jobs are scheduled for Thursday?"
  • "Summarize last month's revenue for me"

The assistant has context about your actual data — clients, jobs, invoices, and expenses. It doesn't just give generic advice; it answers questions specific to your business and your clients' lawns.

Note: The on-device model excels at lawn care knowledge and business queries. For complex legal or tax questions, consult a licensed professional.

Voice Job Entry

Create jobs without typing. Tap the microphone on the Jobs or Schedule screen and describe the job out loud. LawnBook's speech recognition and natural language AI extract all the details:

  • Client name or address
  • Service type (mow, edge, fertilize, aerate, etc.)
  • Date and time
  • Notes or special instructions
  • Estimated duration

The extracted information is shown for your review before saving. Voice entry is available offline and supports natural phrasing — "Schedule a mow and blow for the Johnson property next Monday at 8" works as well as more formal descriptions.

AI Receipt Scanner

Point your camera at any business receipt and the AI extracts the vendor name, total amount, date, and line items. It then suggests the appropriate Schedule C expense category. Receipt images are stored on-device alongside the expense entry as a digital record.

The scanner handles a wide range of receipt formats including gas station printouts, hardware store receipts, supply wholesaler invoices, handwritten receipts, and mobile POS receipts from small suppliers.

Daily Briefing

Each morning, LawnBook generates a Daily Briefing — a short summary of your day based on your schedule, weather data, and open business items. The briefing covers:

  • Jobs scheduled for today and their addresses
  • Weather forecast for your area and any rain risk
  • Outstanding invoices that are overdue
  • Low-stock supply alerts
  • Upcoming quarterly estimated tax due dates
  • Clients who haven't had service in a while

The briefing appears as a notification each morning and is always available from the Dashboard. It's written in plain conversational language, not a wall of numbers.

Tip: Read your daily briefing before you get in the truck. It surfaces things you might otherwise miss — like the fact that one of your Thursday jobs has a client note about a locked gate code you set up last month.

Seasonal Planner

The Seasonal Planner generates a month-by-month lawn care plan for each client based on their property's grass type, region, and current season. Plans include specific recommendations for:

  • Fertilizer application timing and product type
  • Pre-emergent and post-emergent herbicide windows
  • Aeration and overseeding timing
  • Dethatching recommendations
  • Mowing height adjustments by season
  • Irrigation schedule changes

Access the Seasonal Planner from any client's profile page or from the AI tab. Plans can be exported as PDF to share with clients as a professional service proposal.

Lawn Photo Analysis

Take a photo of a lawn and the AI analyzes what it sees. The analysis can identify:

  • Common turf diseases (brown patch, dollar spot, pythium blight)
  • Weed types and suggested treatment
  • Signs of pest damage (grubs, chinch bugs, armyworms)
  • Nutrient deficiency indicators (yellowing patterns, color variation)
  • Drought stress vs. disease stress patterns

Analysis results include a confidence level and suggested next steps. Photos and analysis are stored with the client's service history as documentation. The AI runs entirely on-device — no lawn photos are uploaded to any server.

Tip: Use photo analysis as a first-pass diagnostic. It catches the obvious issues well, but complex or ambiguous cases should still involve a soil test or consultation with a local extension service.

Smart Estimates

When creating a job estimate, the Smart Estimate feature suggests pricing based on the service type, property size, and your historical pricing data for similar jobs. It learns from your actual invoices over time, so suggestions become more accurate the longer you use the app.

Smart Estimates also flag if a proposed price is significantly below your typical rate for that service — a useful check when estimating under pressure on the phone with a new client.

Proposal Generator

Generate a professional service proposal for a new client in under two minutes. The Proposal Generator pulls together:

  • Client and property information
  • Recommended service plan based on grass type and property assessment
  • Itemized pricing for each service
  • Seasonal schedule overview
  • Your business name and contact information

The generated proposal is formatted as a professional PDF you can share via email or text directly from the app. It's polished enough to send to commercial property managers or HOAs, not just residential clients.

On-Device Privacy

Every AI feature described on this page runs entirely on your device using Apple's Core ML framework. LawnBook does not have AI servers. No photos, receipts, voice recordings, or business data are transmitted to process AI requests. This is not a marketing claim — it's a technical architecture decision built into the app from day one.
