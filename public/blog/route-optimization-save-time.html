How Route Optimization Can Save You Hours Every Week

February 1, 2026 6 min read LawnBook Team

There are only so many billable hours in a workday. A lawn care operator servicing 8–12 properties a day can easily spend 1.5 to 2.5 hours in transit between jobs — that's time you're burning fuel, putting miles on your truck, and not generating revenue. Over a 5-day week, that's up to 12.5 hours of unproductive drive time.

Route optimization attacks that number directly. By planning the most efficient path between your jobs before the day starts, you can often cut transit time by 20–40%. That time goes back to you as extra jobs, earlier finish times, or simply less exhaustion at the end of the day.

20–40% Typical reduction in daily drive time
1–2 Extra jobs possible per optimized day
$800+ Monthly fuel savings for a 5-day route

Why "Eyeballing" Your Route Doesn't Cut It

Most operators mentally sequence their jobs based on habit — the order they added clients, the neighborhoods they know well, or rough geography. This works reasonably well, but it leaves time and money on the table every single day.

The human brain is notoriously bad at solving routing problems. What feels like an efficient sequence often has unnecessary backtracking baked in. We tend to overestimate the efficiency of familiar routes and underestimate time lost to indirect paths.

The "Traveling Salesman Problem" — the mathematical challenge of finding the shortest path through multiple points — is a classic computer science problem that gets exponentially harder as stops increase. With 12 job sites, there are billions of possible sequences. Solving it by instinct is essentially impossible. Algorithms handle it in seconds.

What Route Optimization Actually Does

A good routing algorithm considers more than just distance. It accounts for:

  • Geographic clustering: Group nearby properties to minimize cross-town driving
  • Time windows: Respect any fixed-time appointments or client preferences
  • Start and end location: Begin and end at your home base or depot
  • Traffic patterns: Route around predictable congestion where possible
  • Job duration: Factor in actual service time at each property

The result is a sequenced list of stops in the order that minimizes total drive time — not just distance, but actual time spent behind the wheel across a full day.

The Real Financial Impact

Shaving 45 minutes off your daily drive time doesn't just feel better — it compounds into significant money over a season.

Fuel savings: If you're driving 60 fewer miles per day in a truck that gets 15 MPG at $3.50/gallon, that's $14 saved per day. Over 200 working days, that's $2,800 back in your pocket annually — just from gas.

Vehicle wear: Fewer miles means lower maintenance costs, slower depreciation, and a longer useful life for your truck and trailer. The average cost per mile for a pickup truck — including fuel, tires, maintenance, and depreciation — is $0.40 to $0.70. Cutting 60 miles a day saves $24–$42 daily.

Additional revenue: If recovered drive time lets you fit one extra $75 mowing job per day, that's $375 per 5-day week — $15,000 over a 40-week season. Route optimization pays for itself within the first week.

Practical Tips to Maximize Route Efficiency

  1. Group clients geographically when scheduling. When onboarding new clients, prioritize filling in gaps near your existing clusters rather than adding isolated stops on the edges of your service area.
  2. Set a consistent start location. Always begin your route from the same point so the algorithm can optimize the full path accurately.
  3. Build in buffer time for first-visit properties. New clients often take longer than estimated. Account for that in your schedule so a single delay doesn't cascade.
  4. Run your route the night before. Don't waste morning time planning. Have your sequence locked in before you load the truck.
  5. Re-optimize when jobs cancel or get added. A cancellation that leaves a gap in your route can be filled — or the surrounding jobs can be resequenced to eliminate the dead-end drive. Rerun optimization any time your day changes.
